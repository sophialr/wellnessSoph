When daily life gets disrupted, it is quite unsettling. We realize we take for granted running for the bus to make it to class, trekking through snow, showing up to class half asleep to have our friends laugh at us in person, commuting to work to see colleagues, having the option to get out and go wherever. Life is not normal right now. There is so much uncertainty and it does not feel right. However, we are in it together and luckily we live in a day where technology makes it possible to stay connected.

This time is weird and different, but we are staying isolated to ensure our health and safety to get back to the normal life we miss so much. We are in it together and can get through it together. As time progresses, life at home will develop some semblance of a routine. We do not know how long this will last, but trying to form a routine for this new lifestyle is best. In order to stay active during this stressful time, I am here to help share ideas on how to incorporate a good, satisfying, endorphin releasing workout into your new routine!

So many companies, bloggers, and programs have developed at home workouts and live streams to follow. Getting a workout in will help to get your sweat in. Not being able to leave the house to go to class has made me more eager to get my steps in by walking the dog or running. I can‚Äôt sit all day and need a change of scenery. Whether an actual workout is right for you, live stream yoga, walk around the block don‚Äôt forget to get your move on. Moving and waking up the body is how to maintain your health. Don‚Äôt lose it!

Also, you could get creative with new hobbies!

Once you have settled into your routine, you no longer have to worry about commuting anywhere. Use that extra time and energy to get into a new hobby! Discover your love for baking, learn how to code, read a book, join a virtual book club, sew, watch a Netflix series, watch movies, catch up with old friends, journal, try yoga, do abs everyday, clean, find your ideal job (if unhappy), do some research, cook dinner for people sharing your quarantine space, walk everyday (if possible), meditate, play board games, the options are endless!

Take, for example, my friend Kimberely. She loves to bake, but the oven in her apartment at school was not suitable. She got into making pies last month, but now being home she can continue to make her pies without the oven being a problem. Not only is she able to bake but she is making an impact on the community. She asks friends and family if they want a baked good that she will drop off at their house. Sometimes she might even surprise you and drop off something sweet when not expecting it -thank you ;)! She is very careful to keep her cooking environment and delivery trips very clean. This quarantine has let her dive back into her love for baking and spread the happiness around the community at the same time. Love this!!

Don't forget to stay virtually connected with friends. Being isolated and only focused on school work or work is not good. Remember what makes you happy although you may feel/are trapped inside. Getting outside and getting some fresh air is a good step to feeling better. A subscription to headspace sounds like a great idea now.
